Social Studies Major

The Social Studies major at Ersanawata School offers students an in-depth exploration of the complex dynamics of human society, behavior, and the environment. This program is designed to provide a well-rounded education that integrates various disciplines, helping students develop a comprehensive understanding of the world around them. Students will engage with a range of fascinating subjects, including Sociology, which delves into social interactions, community behavior, and societal structures. In Geography, they will study Earth's physical characteristics, natural phenomena, and the relationship between humans and their environment. Economics introduces the principles of production, consumption, and distribution, along with foundational knowledge of accounting, equipping students with financial literacy skills. The program also emphasizes the importance of History, where students explore significant past events, cultural developments, and their impact on modern society. While Mathematics remains a core part of the curriculum, the focus is tailored to practical applications for the Social Studies field. Additionally, students enhance their communication skills through Indonesian Language and English Language studies, preparing them for academic and professional success in a globalized world. Ersanawata School's Social Studies major is committed to fostering critical thinking, analytical skills, and a deep understanding of social issues, making it an excellent choice for students eager to pursue careers in law, education, business, journalism, or public administration. Through this program, students gain the knowledge and skills to contribute meaningfully to society while shaping their future aspirations.

SOCIAL STUDIES SUBJECT

Sociology

Sociology is a specialized subject that you will encounter during your high school education in the Social Studies (IPS) stream. This subject focuses on the study of society. You will explore topics such as individual interactions, societal structures, and social behavior patterns within various communities. Sociology also delves into social issues, cultural norms, and collective actions, equipping students with an understanding of how human relationships and institutions shape the world. This subject is essential for those interested in careers related to social services, community development, or public policy.

Geography

Geography is another core subject in the Social Studies stream, studied from grades 10 to 12. It is the science that teaches you about the physical characteristics of the Earth, as well as the processes and phenomena that occur within it. You will learn about the interaction between humans and their environment, including topics such as spatial variations, natural disasters, ecosystems, and global climate patterns. Geography also covers the study of human populations, migration, urbanization, and their impact on the planet. It’s a valuable subject for understanding the relationship between humans and the Earth and is foundational for careers in urban planning, environmental science, and geopolitics.

Economics

Economics is a subject that introduces students to the study of human activities related to the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. This subject provides insights into fundamental economic principles, such as supply and demand, market structures, and national economic systems. Additionally, you will learn about financial management, including accounting—a vital component of economics. Through accounting lessons, you will gain skills in planning, evaluating, and auditing financial records. These topics include financial statements, balance sheets, and business management processes. Economics serves as a foundation for pursuing further education and careers in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and public administration.

History

Although history is not typically included in national exams, it is a core subject in the Social Studies stream. History provides students with an understanding of past events, including human achievements, cultural developments, and significant historical discoveries. This subject explores the lives of historical figures, ancient civilizations, revolutions, and cultural transformations that have shaped modern society. Studying history helps students develop critical thinking and analytical skills while fostering an appreciation for the complexities of human progress. History is indispensable for careers in education, research, museology, and cultural preservation.

Mathematics

Students in the Social Studies stream still study mathematics, though the content is less complex compared to that in the Science stream. Social Studies mathematics focuses on practical applications, such as statistics, basic algebra, and financial calculations, which are directly relevant to everyday life and various career paths. The subject is designed to equip students with problem-solving skills and analytical thinking, essential for fields such as economics, business, and social research.

Indonesian Language

From grades 10 to 12, students in the Social Studies stream will study the Indonesian language. This subject aims to enhance students' ability to communicate effectively in Bahasa Indonesia, both in written and spoken forms. The curriculum covers grammar, essay writing, and public speaking, ensuring students are well-prepared for national exams. The Indonesian language course is also crucial for fostering cultural awareness and national identity.

English Language

Similar to the Indonesian language, English is a required subject for Social Studies students. The English curriculum focuses on developing skills in grammar, reading comprehension, writing, and conversation. Students are encouraged to achieve proficiency in English, as it is an international language that opens doors to global opportunities. Mastery of English is particularly valuable for higher education and future careers in fields such as international relations, tourism, or multinational business.
